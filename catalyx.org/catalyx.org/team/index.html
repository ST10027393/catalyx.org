JOE WHITE

DIRECTOR

I am a social entrepreneur, and I discovered my passion when I moved to South Africa from California, USA in 1996. This passion has led me to establish 9 organizations, most of which exist today. These organizations include Catalyx (design and management of social impact projects), World Changers Academy (leadership, life skills training, job placement) and VukAfrica Tours (cultural tourism) and others.

 

I sincerely believe in mentoring people to help them reach their full potential and as such I have supported many budding entrepreneurs. I am passionate about community and my work premises on finding solutions to combat social challenges such as unemployment and enterprise development.

 

“Investing in others is also an investment in yourself”

I am passionate about “Engaging Kingdom leaders that are making a difference”

SENIOR PROJECT MANAGER

Nontobeko Nzama

Afro’s Chicken Pavilion Managing Director

Nontobeko (Nonto) is a young entrepreneur, who has spent most of her career in the hospitality sector at junior and management levels. She has also worked as a cleaner, an electrician’s assistant, a promoter and a model. She hails from humble beginnings in KwaNgcolosi in the Valley of 1000 Hills and was proud to have built her own home when she was only 23 years old through hard work and perseverance. 10 years later, she fulfilled her dream of entrepreneurship by opening her first restaurant, Afro’s Chicken at The Pavilion Shopping Centre, in Durban, in partnership with Joe White and Catalyx.

 

She believes that “You should leave footprints of kindness where ever you go. Rainbows will follow.”
